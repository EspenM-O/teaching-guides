FCS Teaching Topics: Confessions of a Converted Lecturer

TAKE-AWAYS

Methods That Work in Class
  1. Shift the focus from "teaching" to helping students learn. What do they need in order to learn?
  2. Post a copy of the lecture content beforehand (post on Brightspace - all faculty and students have access to this LMS at Dalhousie University). Therefore, students can actually engage with the information during the lecture instead of just note taking.
  3. Peer to peer instruction - allow students to work through problems together to assist in the learning process and consolidate skills learnt in class.
Factors That Inhibit Student Learning
  1. Frustration
  2. Boredom
  3. Lack of opportunity to engage with course content in class
`

What are the Features of Peer Instruction?

  1. Pre-class reading
  2. In-class focus on depth, not "coverage"
  3. Give students responsibility for gathering information (give them some content and ask them to prepare a lesson on it)
  4. Use Concept Tests (short, informal, targeted tests -for example Top Hat- that are administered during class to help instructors gauge whether students understand key concepts. They can be used both to assess students' prior knowledge (coming into a course or unit) or their understanding of content in the current course/class. (Mazur, 2012)

I've been teaching for a long time, why should I change what I've been doing?

“If we teach today’s students as we taught yesterday’s, we rob them of tomorrow.” ― John Dewey
It is important to note that not all students learn the way we prefer to teach! We must do things differently to provide our course content in different ways to reach our learners.
There are many factors to consider when addressing how students learn. Some of those factors are up to the students themselves, such as good study habits, note taking and being responsible to research additional information on their own. However, the learning environment in any given class is up to the instructor to create.
When we instruct students, we must ensure not only that content we are teaching is transferred to the student, but that it is understood in a way that is meaningful. Information becomes knowledge and engaging with the content of a lecture or class should translate into not only knowledge itself, but skills. How to apply the content learned to solve a problem or question. In essence, synthesis of the information and appropriate application.

What can I do differently?

The Lecture
  • Provide enough quality content on slides or handouts
  • Transparent links to learning goals (Kozimar-King & Chin, 2018)
  • Do Not Rely on Only Talking - use visuals!
  • Use contextual examples, students will make mental connections between the theory and its application
  • Use Active Learning Techniques - ideas can be found on p.3: FOCUS: Centre for Learning and
  • Peer Instruction (Mazur, 2012)
