FCS Teaching Topics:UDL (Universal Design for Learning) Part 1 of 3

TAKE-AWAYS

UDL is an educational framework and set of principles that maximizes learning opportunities for all learners (Durham College, 2019). It is based on three main principles that should be considered when designing and teaching a course:
  • Multiple means of representation - give learners various ways of acquiring information and knowledge
  • Multiple means of expression - provide learners alternatives for demonstrating what they know
  • Multiple means of engagement - tap into learners' interests, offer appropriate challenges, and increase motivation
For the the purpose of this sway, we will focus on "multiple means of representation".

Why should we provide multiple means of representation?

Not all students learn in the same way. UDL focuses on reducing BARRIERS to learning. We have a wide variety of learners who need to access course content in specific ways for their needs (Cast 2019), such as:
  1. Students who have vision or hearing impairments
  2. Second language learners
  3. International students
  4. Students with learning disabilities
  5. Students with various learning preferences
  6. Students with mental health challenges
Ultimately, we want our students to be successful, and we also want to be effective as educators. By expressing our course content in various ways we reduce barriers for our students, facilitate access to course content and provide various methods to engage with learning material that can benefit our learners.
It is important to note that small, but significant changes can be implemented without increasing your workload.

Easy changes that can benefit your learners:

  • Wear a microphone to be easily heard in lectures
  • Use speech-to-text in lectures over Powerpoint slides, to assist second language learners or those who may have auditory issues
  • Provide visual diagrams, charts and infographics instead of text only
  • Post lecture slides or lecture notes online for learners to access before/ after classes
  • Post relevant multi-media about course content. ie., videos about the topic or video tutorials (students have the benefit of pausing and replaying to improve their understanding)
  • Record your lecture and post it - if you dare! It can be daunting but students who have missed a lecture due to illness etc. will still be able to access it! Saving you time from explaining what was covered.
