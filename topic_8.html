FCS Teaching Topics: UDL (Universal Design for Learning) Part 2 of 3

Take-Aways

UDL is an education framework and set of principles that maximizes learning opportunities for all learners (Durham College, 2019). It is based on three main principles that should be considered when designing and teaching a course:
  • Multiple means of representation- give learners various ways of acquiring information and knowledge
  • Multiple means of actions and expression- provide learners alternatives for demonstrating what they know
  • Multiple means of engagement- tap into learners' interests, offer appropriate challenges and increase motivation
For the purpose of this page, we will focus on "multiple means of action and expression."

Methods of Action and Expression

  • Student can use alternatives to handwriting, such as: typing, audio, voice to text or digital inking
  • Using multiple media for communication, social media, audio, text, video, comic strips, animations (Center for Excellence in Teaching and Learning at OU, 2017).
  • Use scaffolding techniques for tasks (break tasks down into manageable chunks)
  • Provide differentiated models to emulate (i.e. models that demonstrate the same outcomes but use differing approaches, strategies, skills, etc.) (Cast, 2018)

Action and Expression - Main Themes

(Cast, 2018b): http://udlguidelines.cast.org/action-expression
Physical Action - Interact with accessible materials and tools
  • Vary the methods for response and navigation
  • Optimize access to tools and assistive technologies
Expression & Communication - Compose and share ideas using tools that help attain learning goals
  • Use multiple media for communication
  • Use multiple tools for construction and composition
  • Build fluencies with graduated levels of support for practice and performance
Executive Functions - Develop and act on plans to make the most out of learning
  • Guide appropriate goal-setting
  • Support planning and strategy development
  • Facilitate managing information and resources
  • Enhance capacity for monitoring progress
