FCS Teaching Topics: Top 10 Tips for Teaching Assistants and Markers

TAKE-AWAYS

Teaching Assistants and Markers provide vital support to faculty at universities. To ensure they are as effective as possible, the elements below should be considered and implemented:
  1. Job description - Spend time to provide them with a clear description of exactly what you need them to do verbally, provide a soft and hard copy that they can go back and refer to. It's also important that they understand what authority they do and do not have in the classroom, labs, assessing work and dealing with students.
  2. Training - it is extremely important that they are trained properly. Even if a TA or marker has prior experience with teaching courses, delivery of content, use of Brightspace, varying marking strategies etc. there is probably something new for your TA or marker to learn for your needs and context. Ensure TAs must the training offered by the faculty.
  3. Communication - meeting face-to-face with your TAs and markers and establishing regular written communication will help to ensure that everyone is on task, and that the information that you want them to know has been received and understood.
  4. Supportive environment - develop a collegial, kind and approachable relationship between you individually and among your TAs and Markers as a group. This will assist in developing a support network so the onus isn't always on you! Saving you time!
  5. Expectations - have high expectations! Set time lines for when work must be submitted and follow up to ensure everyone is on task.
  6. Feedback - both TAs and Markers need to develop "feedback language" whether its verbal or written, keeping it timely, relevant to the task and focused on what students need to do to improve their work.
  7. Language Barriers and Cultural Differences - Be mindful that many of our students are NOT native English speakers and are international students. Cultural and linguistic differences should be recognized. So, it's tremendously important that communication and training are delivered with this in mind. Demonstrate and cultivate the interpersonal skills that are needed for the job! Provide guidance and link to Dal's policy on the Diversity and Inclusiveness Strategy: Strategic Priority 5.2: Foster a collegial culture grounded in diversity and inclusiveness
  8. Flowchart - understanding processes of "how things work" is important! Whether that is for dealing with challenges with students or timelines and processes of work. Organizational flowcharts are also very helpful as they provide a visual for who needs to be contacted when/if certain scenarios occur. Sometimes TAs and Markers will be able to deal with issues themselves and other times they will need to manage the issues up to someone with more authority.
  9. Getting paid - students are on a budget and are relying on getting paid on time. Not having access to funds on time causes enormous amounts of stress. Have all of your forms submitted to Christian Blouin for initial approval, then marker time sheets must be sent directly to timesheets@cs.dal.ca for payroll - so that students get paid for the work they have done on time. You can assign a Head TA to manage and process this for you.
  10. The ultimate goal - is to foster an environment for LEARNING to take place! TAs and Markers must adapt what they do if learning isn't happening.
